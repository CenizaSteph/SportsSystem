TEAM SPORTS SCHEDULING SYSTEM ADVANTAGES

Effective Organization:

Sports management system can help to quickly prepare games ahead of time.
Future games, prior game results, season records, and any other team specifics may be all found in one spot.


Streamlined player registration process:

It enable organizations to manage the registration process more tightly to achieve the desired results,
including recruiting the right players, improving registrations and revenues.


Team Management made easy:

It enables managing team schedules, messages and team resources.
It helps to simplify the team management and communication, which leads to better teamwork and team success.


Efficient scheduling (League Tournament):

A modern platform should allow generating the league schedules,
and tournament brackets with sophisticated options by avoiding all conflicts.


Facility management made easy:

It allows staff members to manage the procurement process, allocation of the fields,
and make it easy to access all the details of the facility, including pictures, maps, parking details.


Publishing website and mobile app made easy and efficient:

Automatically publish information online like programs for registration,
articles, schedules, standings, brackets, documents, fields, staff members, details, online shop, etc.


Make it easy to pay:

Allows organization staff to streamline payment collection,
up to date record keeping, payment reports, and payment reminders.

FUNCTIONS OF TEAM SPORTS SCHEDULING SYSTEM

Identification phase:

the user of the system will need to provide the name of the teams that will be involved in the tournament. Then, the system will automatically check for the duplicate teams’ name (if any). If the duplication occurs, the system will require the user to insert the team’s name again before allowing proceeding to the next step.

Data Input and Sources:

When direction connections (application programming interface) between a team’s database and the league or a website’s database are not available, web harvesting or scraping techniques can be explored to automate and facilitate one-time data extraction or regular feeding from online servers. Considering the fixed and dynamic constraint.

Fixed constraints:

Game location, opponents’ team, dates, times, and phase of the season (pre-season, regular season, playoffs finals, post-season) are defined by the official competitive.

Dynamic constraints:

Game difficulty can be developed internally as a sub-model within the scheduling decision support system, or retrieved from public sources.
Daily roster availability can be retrieved from the team's athlete management system (AMS) or manually entered before the upcoming practice or game.
